Funding & visa

Scholarships, funding & the US F-1 visa

Two parallel tracks — how to fund the degree, and how to secure your stay.

Scholarships & funding

  • Fulbright Foreign Student Program — flagship US government award.
  • Inlaks Shivdasani & Tata Scholarships for Indian students.
  • University fellowships, assistantships (TA/RA), and need-based aid.
  • Departmental research funding — especially for PhD applicants.
  • Education loans through Indian and US lenders.

Student visa snapshot

  • Visa name: F-1 Student Visa.
  • Requires Form I-20 from a SEVP-certified institution after admission.
  • SEVIS fee + visa application fee, plus consular interview.
  • Strong financial proof of tuition + living expenses for first year.
  • On-campus work allowed up to 20 hours/week during term.
Timeline

Application timeline

A typical 14–18 month roadmap for a Fall intake.

Profile review & test plan

14–18 months out: clarify direction, pick test (GRE/GMAT/SAT/ACT), assess profile.

Standardised tests

10–14 months out: TOEFL/IELTS plus GRE, GMAT, SAT, or ACT depending on program.

Essays, LORs, applications

August–December: SOPs, recommendations, transcripts, and submissions.

Decisions & financial aid

January–April: review admits, funding offers, and finalize school.

I-20 & F-1 visa

April–July: receive I-20, pay SEVIS, complete visa interview.

Pre-departure & arrival

July–August: housing, banking, insurance, flight, campus arrival.
